Synthetic Data v.s. Realistic Data

Synthetic Data: The data was generated by the existing LLM or MLLM. In contrast, The synthetic data has a uniform format, which will lead to pattern overfitting and cause a significant shift in the output distribution.

Realistic Data: The data is collected from the real world, which can alleviate the limitations of synthetic data. Substantial unpair data in the real world has not been excavated completely. Therefore, we propose our pipeline and method to help generate pair data from the real world, and we believe that the final step towards AGI needs to use real-world data.

Key Findings

  • Topic-based Assesment: Notably, samples related to flower and automotive constitute only 0.4\% and 0.9\% of the dataset. Due to the low number of samples, the model fails to adequately learn these concepts, resulting in poor linear probe and zero-shot transfer performance on the Flowers and Cars dataset.
  • Richness Accessment: Both the retrieved real-world sentences and semantic augmented synthetic captions encompass a larger quantity of words, which can provide extensive textual information and improve vision-language pre-training.
  • Diversity Accessment: Both the most relevant retrieved real-world sentence and semantic augmented synthetic caption of the RW-KRA contain a larger number of distinct entities. This diversity of the data effectively guides the model in learning richer knowledge, thereby improving the performance and robustness.

Key Findings

  • Model Size Impact: Larger language models (as measured by MTEB benchmark) consistently achieve better alignment with the vision anchor, highlighting the importance of model scale.
  • Language Understanding Critical: Strong language understanding capabilities are essential for complex vision-language reasoning tasks.
  • CLIP Training Limitations: Training text encoders solely through CLIP-style contrastive learning proves insufficient for optimal performance.
  • Pretrained LM Advantage: Leveraging pretrained language models as text encoders emerges as a promising strategy for building robust vision-language models, as they bring rich linguistic knowledge.

Part 2: Methodology

Real-World Knowledge Extraction Pipeline

To explore the utilization of real-world knowledge extracted from interleaved image-text documents for vision-language pre-training, we establish a Real-World Knowledge Extraction pipeline to extract high-quality images and textual sentences from interleaved documents to separate databases. This pipeline mainly consists of three steps: Data Extraction, Visual Image Filtration, and Textual Knowledge Filtration.

Figure: The Real-World Knowledge Extraction pipeline to extract high-quality images and textual sentences from interleaved documents to separate databases.

Steps

  • Data Extraction: We initially select a random sample of 80 million interleaved image-text documents from the OBELICS as our primary data source. Then, we extract all images and store them in a dedicated image database. Concurrently, we use the Natural Language Toolkit(NLTK) to segment the sentences and store them in a separate sentence database.
  • Visual Image Filtration: We first remove images under any of the following conditions: 1. the shorter dimension is less than 100 pixels; 2. the aspect ratio exceeds 3 or is less than 1/3. This step eliminates 30 million low-quality images. Following CLIP-CID, we employ the EVA02-CLIP E/14-plus model to extract image embeddings and use the Union-Find algorithm to filter out perceptually and semantically redundant images.
  • Textual Knowledge Filtration: These sentences undergo rigorous filtering based on quality, semantics, and redundancy. Initially, we eliminate sentences based on the following criteria: 1. presence of emojis or URLs; 2. sentences containing fewer than 3 or more than 81 words; and 3. following CAT~\cite{CAT_rule}, we retain samples with at least C1 caption complexity and incorporating an action.

Real-World Knowledge Retrieval and Augmentation

We propose a Real-World Knowledge Retrieval and Augmentation framework to efficiently retrieve the three most semantically relevant for each image and leverage large language models to integrate real-world knowledge with image semantic information and generate synthetic captions. The architecture of our framework primarily consists of three components: Hierarchical Knowledge Retrieval, Image Semantic Enhancement, and Knowledge Augmentation.

Figure: The architecture of our proposed Real-World Knowledge Retrieval and Augmentation framework, which converts extracted real-world knowledge to distinct image-text pairs via retrieval and augmentation.

Key Contributions

  • Hierarchical Knowledge Retrieval: We leverage the EVA02-CLIP E/14-plus model to encode all the images and sentences. Inspired by Unicom, we utilize the standard K-means algorithm offline to partition the 0.8B sentences into 2M clusters with the help of efficient feature quantization. Due to the substantial computational cost (over ten thousand hours on 8 $\times$ A100 GPUs) of directly selecting the three most relevant sentences from 0.8B sentences for an image, we divide the process into two levels to optimize retrieval efficiency. We first perform inter-cluster retrieval to find the most relevant cluster center for each image. Then, we group images sharing the same cluster center and perform intra-cluster retrieval to obtain the three most semantically relevant sentences. This approach enables the retrieval of 99 million images and 0.8 billion sentences within 20 hours using 8 A100 GPUs.
  • Image Semantic Enhancement: The above retrieval process cannot see the image content. To enrich image semantic information, we introduce the Image Semantic Enhancement module. This module adopts the OFA~\cite{OFA} model to generate a brief caption for each image. To obtain more fine-grained semantic information, we incorporate the open-set image tagging model RAM++ to extract object detection tags. Since RAM++ supports only 4K tags, we extend the tag support to 8K by collecting an additional 4K tags from real-world sentences and employing ChatGPT4-Turbo to generate corresponding descriptions.
  • Knowledge Augmentation: After retrieving real-world sentences and acquiring semantic information from images, following CapsFusion, we employ ChatGPT4-Turbo to integrate the first retrieved real-world sentences with image semantics information to generate synthetic captions. We construct a 100K instruction dataset (the prompt we used is presented in the supplementary materials). Subsequently, we employ the LLaMA Factory to fine-tune the LLaMA3-8B model and use vLLM for large-scale inference. Finally, we convert the images and real-world knowledge extracted from 118M interleaved documents to 99M image-text pairs, with each image paired with three retrieved real-world sentences and one semantic augmented synthetic caption.
